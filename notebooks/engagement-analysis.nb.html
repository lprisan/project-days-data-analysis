One thing to note: on the 2017-10-11 event, the observation of technology and resources was conflated into a single observer tick â€“ the current preprocessing assumes that the tick means â€œengaged with technology AND resourcesâ€ (and the absence of tick, not engaged with either technology or resources)

We can see that different dates have quite different engagement profiles, and the same can be said for the aggregation of every kind of project day: e.g., in Keha there was more use of technology and resources, while in Linnaruum there was more talking. However, this is not so easy to interpret, especially for a teacher concerned by the question: â€œare my students engaged?â€

A potential way of answering this with MCA analysis:

obsdata %>% dplyr::select(date, project, disengaged:external) %>% 
  aggregate(.~date+project,data=., mean) %>%
  dplyr::mutate(date=as.factor(date)) %>%
  melt(id.vars=c("date","project")) %>%
  ggplot(aes(date, value)) + 
  geom_bar(aes(fill=variable), position = "dodge", stat = "identity") + theme_minimal()

obsdata %>% dplyr::select(project, disengaged:external) %>% aggregate(.~project,data=., mean) %>%
  melt(id.vars="project") %>%
  ggplot(aes(project, value)) + 
  geom_bar(aes(fill=variable), position = "dodge", stat = "identity") + theme_minimal()

  • MCADim1 explains most (25%) of the variance, and it seems very correlated with disengagement behavior (and somewhat with more passive forms of engagement like looking), and viceversa: negative values are related with the other, more active kinds of engagement (external, resources, technology, talking). We can interpret â€“MCADim1 as â€œActive engagementâ€.
  • â€“MCADim2 could be related with some kind of â€œPassive engagementâ€ (introspection?), since it is quite correlated with looking and talking (but not use of external, techology, or disengagement).
  • MCADim3 is related with external and looking, maybe in the sense of â€œconfusion and help-seekingâ€? (but this is maybe a stretch)
